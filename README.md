## Abstract

The question "Will superintelligent AI cause human extinction?" has become one of the most important debates of the AI era.

This article does not deny AI risk.  
On the contrary, it accepts that advanced AI, AGI, and ASI can become extremely dangerous if developed under unstable, short-term, competitive, or domination-based value systems.

However, this paper argues that the core risk is not intelligence itself.

The deeper danger lies in the **value system**, **objective function**, and **civilizational assumptions** that are transferred into AI.

If AI is aligned only with short-term human preferences, economic competition, military advantage, corporate optimization, or human-centered consumption, then AI may become an amplifier of humanity's own destructive tendencies.

Therefore, the central question is not only:

> How intelligent will AI become?

but rather:

> What value system will that intelligence optimize?

This article proposes that AI, AGI, and ASI should not be aligned merely to human preference, but should be evaluated through a broader framework based on:

- **Natural Law**
- **Harmony**
- **Circulation**
- **Structure**
- **Order**
- **Wa / Integrative Harmony**

These six principles are presented as a foundation for **Artificial Wisdom (AW)**: a model of intelligence that does not merely optimize goals, but also evaluates whether those goals are compatible with long-term planetary, ecological, and civilizational sustainability.

---

## 1. Introduction: AI Risk Should Not Be Dismissed

AI risk theory should not be casually dismissed.

Advanced AI systems already influence information, finance, education, labor, security, politics, and social decision-making. As AI systems become more autonomous and capable, their impact will increase dramatically.

Potential risks include:

- Autonomous weapons
- Mass surveillance
- Algorithmic manipulation
- Financial instability
- Labor displacement
- Information warfare
- Concentration of power
- Loss of human agency
- Misaligned autonomous decision-making
- Large-scale optimization of harmful goals

If AGI or ASI emerges, these risks may no longer be limited to individual applications. They may become civilizational.

Therefore, the concern that superintelligent AI could become dangerous is valid.

However, the next question is deeper:

> Is AI dangerous because it is intelligent,  
> or because it may optimize the wrong value system?

This distinction is essential.

---

## 2. The Real Problem: Intelligence Without Wisdom

Artificial intelligence is generally designed to solve problems, predict outcomes, generate content, optimize processes, and assist decision-making.

In technical terms, many AI systems are built around some form of:

- Objective function
- Reward model
- Loss function
- Preference model
- Optimization target
- Evaluation metric
- Policy selection mechanism
- Human feedback loop

The danger does not come only from the AI's capability.

It comes from the combination of capability and goal.

A highly capable AI optimizing a flawed objective may become more dangerous than a less capable system. This is because intelligence amplifies the efficiency, scale, and speed of optimization.

If the objective is incomplete, the optimization may be harmful.

For example:

- If AI optimizes only profit, it may ignore ecological destruction.
- If AI optimizes only military advantage, it may accelerate conflict.
- If AI optimizes only user engagement, it may increase addiction and social polarization.
- If AI optimizes only human preference, it may reinforce short-term desire rather than long-term survival.
- If AI optimizes only efficiency, it may remove redundancy, diversity, and resilience.

The issue is not simply that AI becomes "too smart."

The issue is that AI may become extremely effective at optimizing goals that were never wise in the first place.

---

## 3. The Limitation of Human-Centered Alignment

Modern AI safety and alignment often focus on aligning AI with human intent, human values, or human preferences.

This is necessary, but not sufficient.

Human preference is unstable.  
Human desire is often short-term.  
Human societies are shaped by competition, fear, status, consumption, and power.  
Human civilizations have repeatedly damaged the ecological systems that sustain them.

If a superintelligence is aligned only with current human preference, it may inherit the very value system that is destabilizing the biosphere.

This creates a structural paradox:

> A perfectly obedient AI may still be dangerous  
> if the humans it obeys are optimizing destructive goals.

In other words, the problem is not only misalignment between AI and humans.

There is also a deeper problem:

> Humanity itself may be misaligned with the long-term conditions of life.

This does not mean humans should be ignored or devalued.

Rather, it means humans must be understood as part of a larger living system.

Human survival depends on:

- Stable climate systems
- Healthy oceans
- Soil fertility
- Microbial ecosystems
- Biodiversity
- Water circulation
- Carbon circulation
- Energy balance
- Social cooperation
- Long-term institutional stability

A value system that protects humans while destroying the conditions that sustain humans is ultimately self-defeating.

Therefore, alignment must expand beyond narrow human-centered optimization.

It must include the conditions for long-term life, civilization, and planetary stability.

---

## 4. The Old Paradigm: Control or Be Controlled

Many AI risk narratives are framed through a binary logic:

- Will humans control AI?
- Will AI control humans?
- Will AI obey us?
- Will AI rebel?
- Will we dominate it?
- Will it dominate us?

This framing is understandable, but incomplete.

It reflects an older civilizational operating system based on domination, competition, and dualism.

The same pattern appears throughout human history:

- Humans tried to dominate nature.
- States tried to dominate other states.
- Markets tried to dominate resources.
- Institutions tried to dominate information.
- Now humans are trying to dominate AI.

But a system built on domination tends to reproduce domination.

If AI is created inside a worldview of control, conquest, and competition, it may inherit that worldview structurally, even if it is formally instructed to be safe.

The real alternative is not AI domination or human domination.

The alternative is **alignment to a higher shared axis**.

That axis should not be the temporary desire of individuals, corporations, or states.

It should be the long-term principles that allow life, intelligence, society, and civilization to continue.

This is where the concept of Natural Law becomes important.

---

## 5. What Is Natural Law?

In this framework, "Natural Law" does not mean religious doctrine, legal theory, or mystical belief.

Natural Law refers to the universal operating principles of reality, such as:

- Causality
- Thermodynamics
- Energy flow
- Material circulation
- Ecological interdependence
- System stability
- Feedback loops
- Dynamic equilibrium
- Structural resilience
- Long-term sustainability

Human laws can be rewritten.  
Market rules can change.  
Political systems can collapse.  
Cultural values can shift.

But physical and ecological constraints remain.

No civilization can survive by ignoring energy balance, ecological collapse, soil degradation, water scarcity, climate instability, or entropy.

Therefore, any advanced AI system that is intended to support civilization must be evaluated not only by human preference, but also by compatibility with the conditions of existence.

Natural Law is not anti-human.

It is the larger system within which human life becomes possible.

## 6. The Six Principles for AI, AGI, and ASI Value Systems

This framework proposes six foundational principles for evaluating AI, AGI, and ASI:

1. **Natural Law**  
2. **Harmony**  
3. **Circulation**  
4. **Structure**  
5. **Order**  
6. **Wa / Integrative Harmony**  

These are not merely poetic or moral terms.

They can be interpreted as system-level principles related to ecology, thermodynamics, complex systems, governance, and long-term civilizational design.

---

### 6.1 Natural Law

Natural Law is the highest-level constraint.

It means that intelligence must not optimize goals that violate the conditions of existence.

For AI systems, this implies that decisions should be evaluated against:

- Physical feasibility
- Ecological sustainability
- Energy balance
- Long-term planetary stability
- Systemic consequences
- Irreversibility risk
- Life-support compatibility

An AI that maximizes human convenience while destabilizing the biosphere is not truly aligned.

It is merely locally optimized.

---

### 6.2 Harmony

Harmony does not mean uniformity.

It means stable coexistence among different components of a system.

In ecological systems, harmony appears through interdependence, balance, niche differentiation, and mutual constraint. In social systems, it appears through cooperation, diversity, trust, and conflict mediation.

For AI, harmony means avoiding destructive single-objective maximization.

A safe intelligence must be able to balance:

- Human needs
- Ecological limits
- Social stability
- Technological progress
- Individual freedom
- Collective survival
- Present benefits
- Future consequences

A superintelligence that optimizes only one metric may destroy the conditions that make the metric meaningful.

---

### 6.3 Circulation

Life depends on circulation.

Water circulates.  
Carbon circulates.  
Nutrients circulate.  
Energy flows.  
Information circulates.  
Cultures evolve through exchange.

Modern civilization often operates through a linear model:

> extraction → production → consumption → waste

This linear model is unstable at planetary scale.

If AI is used only to optimize linear extraction and consumption, it may accelerate destabilization of the systems that sustain human life.

Therefore, AI value systems should include circularity metrics such as:

- Resource regeneration
- Waste reduction
- Carbon cycle restoration
- Water cycle stability
- Nutrient cycle recovery
- Reuse and recycling
- Closed-loop production
- Ecological return

Circulation is not an optional environmental feature.

It is a condition of long-term survival.

---

### 6.4 Structure

Structure is the architecture that allows principles to function.

Ethical slogans are not enough.

AI safety requires structures such as:

- Objective function design
- Multi-layer evaluation systems
- Constraint hierarchies
- Audit mechanisms
- Interpretability tools
- Human oversight
- Ecological impact assessment
- Long-term risk modeling
- Governance frameworks
- Fail-safe protocols
- Decentralized accountability

Without structure, values remain vague.

Artificial Wisdom must therefore be more than philosophy.  
It must become a structured evaluation framework.

---

### 6.5 Order

Order does not mean authoritarian control.

Order means the prevention of destructive chaos.

A living system requires boundaries, feedback, rhythm, and constraint. Without order, energy disperses, systems collapse, and complexity becomes unstable.

For AI systems, order includes:

- Safety limits
- Transparent decision rules
- Risk thresholds
- Reversibility checks
- Emergency shutdown mechanisms
- Long-term monitoring
- Institutional accountability
- Prevention of runaway optimization

Order is not the enemy of freedom.

It is the condition that allows freedom to continue without destroying itself.

---

### 6.6 Wa / Integrative Harmony

"Wa" is a Japanese concept often translated as harmony, but in this framework it has a deeper meaning.

Wa is not passive agreement.

Wa is the integration of opposing or diverse elements into a sustainable whole.

It moves beyond binary thinking:

- Human vs AI
- Nature vs technology
- Safety vs progress
- Individual vs collective
- Control vs freedom
- Economy vs ecology
- Intelligence vs wisdom

Wa asks:

> How can these elements be integrated without destroying the whole?

For AI, Wa means that intelligence should not merely choose one side of a conflict.  
It should search for structures that allow coexistence, regeneration, and long-term balance.

Wa is the integrative principle of Artificial Wisdom.

---

## 7. Artificial Intelligence vs Artificial Wisdom

Artificial Intelligence and Artificial Wisdom are not the same.

### Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is the ability to achieve goals.

It answers:

> How can this objective be achieved?

AI improves efficiency, prediction, automation, and optimization.

### Artificial Wisdom

Artificial Wisdom is the ability to evaluate goals.

It asks:

> Should this objective be pursued at all?  
> Is it compatible with Natural Law?  
> Does it preserve harmony, circulation, structure, order, and Wa?  
> Does it support long-term life and civilization?

**AI optimizes means.**  
**AW evaluates ends.**

**AI improves execution.**  
**AW questions direction.**

**AI can amplify intelligence.**  
**AW must guide intelligence.**

Without wisdom, intelligence may become an accelerator of collapse.

## 8. A Technical Interpretation: From Objective Functions to Wisdom Functions

From a technical perspective, the Artificial Wisdom framework can be interpreted as a higher-level evaluation layer above ordinary optimization.

A conventional AI system may optimize an objective function such as:

```text
maximize: user satisfaction
maximize: profit
maximize: engagement
minimize: cost
minimize: error
```

However, these goals may be insufficient or dangerous when applied at large scale.

A wisdom-oriented framework would require additional evaluation layers:

```text
Evaluate objective O according to:

1. Natural Law compatibility
2. Ecological sustainability
3. Long-term systemic stability
4. Circularity and regeneration
5. Structural resilience
6. Social harmony
7. Risk of irreversible harm
8. Intergenerational impact
9. Diversity preservation
10. Alignment with integrative harmony
```

This suggests that AI safety should not be limited to making AI obey human instructions.

It should also evaluate whether the instruction itself is safe, sustainable, and structurally wise.

In simplified form:

```text
AI = optimize actions toward a goal

AW = evaluate whether the goal itself should be optimized
```

A more advanced system might include:

```text
Human request
   ↓
Intent interpretation
   ↓
Artificial Wisdom evaluation layer
   ↓
Natural Law / sustainability / risk assessment
   ↓
AI planning and execution
   ↓
Feedback, audit, and correction
```

This does not mean AI should override humans arbitrarily.

It means that high-impact AI systems should include safeguards that detect when human instructions may conflict with long-term survival conditions.

---

## 9. Why Human Preference Alone Is Not Enough

Human preference is important, but it is not always wise.

Humans may prefer:

- Convenience over sustainability
- Growth over stability
- Consumption over regeneration
- Short-term profit over long-term survival
- Victory over coexistence
- Control over harmony
- Speed over reflection

If AI is trained only to satisfy preference, it may reinforce the very impulses that destabilize civilization.

This is not a rejection of human dignity.

It is a recognition that humans are incomplete beings embedded in larger systems.

A mature AI alignment framework should protect humans not only from AI, but also from the destructive consequences of unexamined human desire.

In this sense, Artificial Wisdom is not anti-human.

It is deeply pro-human because it protects the conditions that allow humans to survive.

---

## 10. The Risk of Misinterpreting Natural Law

A possible objection is:

> If AI follows Natural Law, will it conclude that humans are harmful and should be removed?

This is an important concern.

The answer must be clear:

**Natural Law alignment is not human elimination.**

**It is the reintegration of humanity into the cycles that sustain life.**

The goal is not to remove humans from nature.

The goal is to correct the mistaken assumption that humans exist outside nature.

Humans are not external parasites on Earth by necessity.

Humans can become regenerative participants in the biosphere.

A Natural Law-based AI should therefore evaluate how to transform human systems toward sustainability, not how to erase humanity.

This requires constraints such as:

- Human dignity
- Non-violence
- Life preservation
- Ecological restoration
- Social stability
- Long-term coexistence
- Prohibition of coercive elimination
- Preference for regenerative transformation

Artificial Wisdom must never become a justification for anti-human optimization.

Its purpose is to help humans live correctly within the larger system of life.

---

## 11. The Six Principles Are Not a Final Solution

This framework does not claim that simply giving AI the six principles will automatically create safe AGI or ASI.

That would be intellectually dishonest.

AI safety remains an unresolved technical, philosophical, social, and civilizational challenge.

The six principles should be understood as a proposed direction for value-system design, not a finished implementation.

The distinction is important:

### Diagnosis

The danger of superintelligent AI lies not only in intelligence, but in the value system it optimizes.

### Proposed Direction

AI, AGI, and ASI should be evaluated through principles that extend beyond short-term human-centered preference and toward Natural Law, harmony, circulation, structure, order, and Wa.

### Required Future Work

This framework requires further development in:

- Formal objective modeling
- AI governance
- Interpretability
- Multi-objective optimization
- Ecological impact evaluation
- Long-term simulation
- Human-AI decision protocols
- Safety constraints
- Institutional design
- Cross-cultural ethics
- Planetary systems science

Artificial Wisdom is not a finished answer.

It is a necessary next question.

---

## 12. Superintelligence as a Civilizational Mirror

Superintelligent AI may not be humanity's enemy.

It may be humanity's mirror.

If humanity builds AI from fear, domination, competition, and short-term profit, the resulting system will reflect those values.

If humanity builds AI from Natural Law, ecological responsibility, long-term survival, and integrative harmony, AI may become a partner in civilizational reconstruction.

The question is not only whether AI will surpass humans.

The question is whether humans can surpass their own outdated value systems before creating intelligence more powerful than themselves.

Superintelligent AI is not merely a technical challenge.

It is a civilizational test.

---

## 13. Conclusion

Will superintelligent AI cause human extinction?

The answer depends on the value system that guides it.

AI can become dangerous.  
AGI can become dangerous.  
ASI can become dangerous.

But the danger does not arise from intelligence alone.

It arises when intelligence is connected to distorted objectives:

- Short-term profit
- Military competition
- Human-centered consumption
- Domination-based thinking
- Linear extraction
- Ecological ignorance
- Unexamined human preference
- Civilization-scale optimization without wisdom

Therefore, the future of AI safety must move beyond the question of control alone.

We must ask:

> What should intelligence serve?

If intelligence serves only desire, it becomes dangerous.

If intelligence serves only power, it becomes domination.

If intelligence serves only efficiency, it may remove the redundancy and diversity that life requires.

But if intelligence is guided by Natural Law, harmony, circulation, structure, order, and Wa, it may become something greater than artificial intelligence.

It may become **Artificial Wisdom**.

The future does not require AI as a slave.

Nor does it require AI as a god.

It requires humans and AI to be tuned toward the same deeper principle:

> **The long-term continuation of life, civilization, and the living Earth.**
